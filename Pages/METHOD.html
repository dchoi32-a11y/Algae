Method



1. Measure the weight of a petri dish.

2. Prepare 20 identical petri dishes and label them according to the four light intensity groups (4, 6, 8, 10 lux).

3. Use measuring cylinders to pour 36 mL of water into each petri dish, ensuring all volumes are equal.

4. Use a pipette to add 10 mL of algae culture into each petri dish so that all samples start with the same amount of algae.

5. Position the light source and adjust the distance or brightness so that each group of petri dishes receives its assigned light intensity.

6. Use a light box to confirm that each group is receiving the correct light intensity (4-10 V).

7. Ensure that all other environmental conditions, such as distance between petri dishes and exposure time, are kept consistent.

8. Allow the algae to grow for a fixed period of time (for example, 5–7 days), keeping the light conditions constant throughout.

9. At the end of the experiment, incubate each algae sample in an incubator to evaporate the water from the biomass.

10. Dry the collected algae using a drying method (such as leaving it in a warm dry place or using a drying oven) until a constant mass is reached.

11. Use an electronic scale to measure the dry mass of algae for each sample.

12. Record all results carefully and calculate the average biomass for each light intensity level.
