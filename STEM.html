Algae: "The Future of Biofuel?"

Research Question:

How does the different light intensity/voltage affect the biomass production of algae (g) for potential Biofuel applications?

Hypothesis:

If light intensity increases (4V~10V), then the algae chlorella vulgaris’s dry mass (g) will increase because higher light levels enhance the rate of photosynthesis.

Background:

Algae are photosynthetic organisms that convert light energy into chemical energy through photosynthesis. Because of their rapid growth and high oil content, algae are considered a promising source of biofuel, a renewable alternative to fossil fuels.
